DevLog 04: A Tour of Dot Hop

Russell Matney

Created: 2024-02-08 Thu 15:18

Intro: a tour of dothop

Dot Hop: a grid based puzzle game

  • you need to hop on every dot from start to end
  • if you get stuck, you can move backwards to undo
  • you can reset the whole puzzle if you’ve made a mess of things
  • the puzzles can get pretty tricky!
  • launching on steam on March 1st
  • hopefully coming to ios/android later this year

Origin story

July of 2023

  • i took a break from godot to try out puzzlescript
  • i’d seen puzzlescript in a video by the good-snowman-is-hard-to-build developer
  • puzzlescript looked amazing! you create one text file and it’s a whole game!
  • i thought it’d help me break out of game engineering and focus on game design
    • (specifically, level design)
  • my main takeaway: plain-text prototyping rules! let’s focus on level design

i thought puzzlescript might be more of a cli tool

  • really it’s just a javascript webapp
  • there are lots of extensions! puzzle-script-plus!

Flower Eater

- submitted to the Fediverse Summer Jam
- I thought the 'dots' were maybe flowers being eaten by this bug
- It was 12 puzzles, including some where you control two bugs

- this game was super accessible! my less gamer friends and family
  were enjoying the first few levels
- some quirks: you can't move backwards, so getting stuck means
  refreshing the browser (or finding the built-in undo button, but
  from my testing it's not clear to players)
- the game did really well! 1st in fun and creativity (out of 7 games....)

so what to do next?

- switched back into overengineering mode, of course
- i wrote a puzzlescript parser into dino (an addon called 'Puzz')
  - note: does not run any puzzlescript game,
    it only parses the plaintext into godot data structures
- i implemented flower eater's rules in gdscript
  - at some point this logic got a test suite, thank god(ot)!
- renamed to dothop
  - so much less to type everywhere!
- i created swappable themes
  - so i could do whatever on top of the game's logic
- i created a steam game (for dino) so I could play it on my steam deck

StrangeLoop play testing

  • I put dot hop in people’s hands, and they wouldn’t give it back
  • maybe there’s something to these puzzles?

Ignoring the quest

  • Knowing that i should get this obvious winner launched as quickly as possible, i….
    • completely dropped it to work on Dino for the entire fall

(vader nooooooo) 

- much of this was worthwhile
  - after dot hop's launch i'll be back on dino full-time
  - the plain-text puzzle generation inspired a similar approach to dino
    - secret project: codename: levelscript (video to come!)

January: refocused on Dot Hop

- steam page and logo
- ripped code out of dino
- new themes, new puzzles
- a 'worldmap' for seeing what puzzles are around
- save/load player progress
- a solver to make sure the puzzles are possible (heh)
- there's even a work-in-progress puzzle generator
  - thanks cameron for the deep dive on this!
- initial sounds and music
- play with one hand!
- animated menus!

This week: Dot Hop is ready for playtesting!

  • are these puzzles too easy or too hard?
    • it takes me ~30 minutes to play through all 50
    • but i have most of them memorized
  • is it juicy enough?
  • i think i need to reward and incentize progress more

Future

  • the ideas keep coming, and i’m fighting scope-creep
  • i’m collecting future ideas in a ’dothop 2’ trello card
  - fog of war
  - endless mode
  - multiplayer
  - a proc-gen world map with doors and keys
  - time trials and leader boards
  - puzzles as rooms on each floor of a rougelike dungeon

yep, lots of scope creep
